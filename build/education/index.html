Codio in Education : support our campaign

Most school curricula focus on using Word, Excel and office productivity tools - things that kids these days pick up in 5 minutes without any instruction. Governments are now trying to put in place curricula that incorporate coding. It's quite easy to imagine how a committee designed curriculum might look. Not compatible with the dynamic world of IT and especially not when it comes to learning to code. By the time a curriculum has been worked through and published, large chunks will be out of date and it will be static for years to come.

We propose an alternative solution that is more fit for purpose - letting the community take charge of its own destiny. We propose that the world's coders, working together, can do a better job of providing the learning materials, infrastructure and motivation needed to train the next generation of coders. We might be wrong, but we believe this 'crowd-teaching' approach is a better solution to the problem that one designed by committee.

We are playing our own small part by providing part of the infrastructure that can support this approach, but we need the community and industry to get behind the idea too. And to shout us down too if you feel we're way off the mark.

Read our more detailed blog post A post-curriculum approach to teaching programming.

Codio:Annotations

Codio:Annotations is one of the main drivers of our educational efforts and will be released before the end of April 2013.

The best place to find out more is to visit the Codio:Annotations documentation where you can get an overview or dive into real detail.

Curation

Codio will soon be adding a front-end layer that allows the community to collaborate in a Wiki like way on the way that teaching materials are structured and organized. Tutorials can be assigned to places within the curation structure so they can be found easily. We will also offer voting and commenting features so the best tutorials can float to the top and improved through feedback.

The GitHub Project

GitHub is the clear leader in open source collaboration projects. Codio will soon be setting up a GitHub project that will be a goto place for tutorials of any kind. It is designed to work fully independently of Codio but the Codio curation features will provide a very friendly front-end for students and learners.

Educode License

We are creating a special license that we will recommend all tutorials and Codio:Annotation projects use. The purpose of this license is to make sure everything is completely open, copyable and leaves people free to improve all available materials in any way they see fit.

Inspire Program

With the Inspire Program, we will recruit renowned coders from all the world to deliver short, informal, ad hoc talks that aim to inspire the next generation of programmers. These will be in video format and will be published under the Educode License so teachers and collaborators are free to do with them whatever they like.

Hope the guys at code.org don't mind us using their image. We're all pulling in the same direction when it comes to inspiration.
